## Revenue objective
Convert Nicholas from “interested in a chatbot” into a paid 14-day pilot, not another free prototype loop.

## Offer to put on the table
**14-day Outdoor Squad enquiry pilot**

Includes:
- website chat assistant tuned to Outdoor Squad tone and FAQs
- lead qualification for goals, fitness level, location, availability, and preferred next step
- free intro class / coach-call handoff CTA
- contact capture and lead notification handoff
- objection handling for beginner nerves, group training hesitation, schedule uncertainty, and price/timetable questions
- nutrition/PT routing only when relevant
- one refinement pass after real conversations

## Founder-friendly pilot pricing
Use one simple anchor, then stop talking:

> “For the first version I’d keep it lean: $299 setup for the 14-day pilot, then if it’s useful we can keep optimizing it for $149/month. If it doesn’t feel useful after the pilot, no ongoing commitment.”

If he hesitates on price:

> “We can make the pilot even narrower: just website FAQs + qualification + free intro handoff for $199, then add nutrition/PT routing later if it earns its keep.”

Do **not** offer more free build work unless he provides the Zoovr chat plus a booked follow-up date.

## Discovery questions that directly support the close
Ask only what affects the pilot scope:
1. Where do enquiries currently arrive: website, Instagram, phone, ads, or referrals?
2. What is the one action you most want a new lead to take: free intro class, booked call, or form submission?
3. Which 3 questions should every lead answer before your team follows up?
4. What are the most common objections before someone tries a class?
5. How will we judge the pilot after 14 days: more free intro bookings, fewer admin replies, faster follow-up, or cleaner lead info?

## Demo path to show
1. “I’m interested but not very fit yet.” → reassure + beginner-friendly positioning.
2. “I work full-time and want evenings.” → qualify goal/schedule + point to free intro.
3. “I’m not sure group training is for me.” → low-pressure objection handling.
4. “Do you help with food?” → nutrition support without pushy upsell.
5. “My name is Sam, 0412 345 678.” → show lead capture/handoff concept.

## Close script
> “Based on this, I think the paid pilot should stay narrow: handle common website enquiries, qualify people properly, and push good-fit leads into the free intro flow. I can tune it around your exact answers and get a first version live quickly. Want to do the 14-day pilot and judge it on real conversations?”

The call is successful only if it ends with one of:
- paid pilot agreed;
- exact pilot scope + price sent + decision date agreed;
- Zoovr chat/questions/CTA received + follow-up date agreed.

Avoid ending with vague enthusiasm.
