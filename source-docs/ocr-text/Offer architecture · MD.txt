The Outdoor Squad — Offer Architecture
Why this document exists
Most fitness businesses make pricing decisions one product at a time and end up with an offer
ladder that's confusing for prospects, hard for staff to explain, and leaks revenue through poorly-
positioned options. The point of this document is to be the single source of truth on what The
Outdoor Squad sells, who each product is for, how the products relate to each other, and how
prospects move through them.
Use this document when:

e Pricing or repricing any product

¢ Designing a launch (the new product needs a place in the ladder)

¢ Briefing the team on how to talk about offers

¢ Training the chatbot on what to recommend to whom

¢ Writing website copy for any product page

e Reviewing whether the ladder is still working (quarterly)
This document captures the offer architecture as of May 2026, immediately following the SPT
and YTP launches and the retirement of Squad Ignite and standalone Nutrition Coaching.
The full offer ladder
Eight products. Four for adults, one for kids, two trial pathways, one for casual drop-ins. Plus 1:1
PT held in reserve as an unmarketed option.

The products in detail
1-Day Free Trial Pass
Role in the ladder: Primary acquisition mechanism. The first promise to a cold prospect.
Whoit's for: Avatar 1 (Desk-Bound Professional) primarily, plus Avatar 3 (Holistic Mover) and
any prospect who needs to "see what it's like" before committing. Avatar 2 (Strength Seeker)
sometimes uses this; more often skips it for the Kickstarter.
What's included: One free class at any standard time slot, both locations. Pre-trial email with
what to expect, what to bring, where to park, who to look for.


--- PAGE 3 ---
What's NOT included: Multiple sessions, nutrition consult, movement screen, SPT.

Conversion target: Trial-to-paid within 14 days. Trial > Squad Ascent is the most common path.
Strategic notes: This is your front door. The trial experience needs to be ruthlessly good — warm
welcome, name remembered, clear next-step conversation at the end. Trial conversion rate is one
of the most important metrics in the whole business; treat it as such.

5-Day High-Protein Australian Meal Plan (lead magnet)

Role in the ladder: Email list builder. Lower commitment than the trial pass, captures prospects
who aren't ready to physically show up yet.

Whoit's for: All avatars at the awareness stage. Particularly strong for Avatar 1 (curious but
anxious) and Avatar 3 (researcher type who wants something useful before committing).
What's included: Downloadable meal plan delivered by email; opt-in to the email nurture
sequence afterwards.

Strategic notes: A lead magnet's job isn't to be perfect — it's to be useful enough that the
prospect feels they got something good, while gently building the case for full membership. The
follow-up email sequence is what converts; the meal plan itself is the door-opener. Both need to
be good. Worth a refresh annually.

Drop-in / Casual Session — $37

Role in the ladder: Friction. Designed not to be cheap.

Who it's for: Visitors to Sydney, lapsed members coming back to test the waters, people whose
schedule genuinely can't accommodate a regular membership.

What's included: One session, any standard time slot.

Strategic notes: $37 looks expensive against $51/wk for 5+ sessions, and that's deliberate. A $37
drop-in price sends the message "the membership is the obvious choice." If casual drop-in
prospects buy it twice, conversation about membership starts.

This is also a useful price anchor. When prospects ask "how much is it?" the high-end honest
answer ($175/wk SPT 3x), the middle answer ($51/wk Squad Ascent), and the casual answer
($37/session) make the membership look like the rational decision before you say a word about
it.

Squad Student — $25/wk

Role in the ladder: Affordable entry for a specific demographic. Pricing access without diluting
the main product.

Whoit's for: Tertiary students (verified). Adjacent demographic to Avatar 2 (some young
Strength Seekers are still students).

What's included: Unlimited group classes — same product as Squad Ascent, different price,
eligibility-gated.

Strategic notes: Students who train at TOS as students tend to graduate into Squad Ascent / SPT
members later in life. The lifetime value of a student member is meaningfully higher than the
weekly revenue suggests. Verify student status to prevent abuse, but otherwise let it run.

YTP (Youth Training Program) — $25/wk

Role in the ladder: A new audience entirely. The parent buys; the kid uses.

Who it's for: Avatar 5 (parent-buyer) of a 10-17-year-old. Often an existing TOS member who
wants their kid to do what they do.

What's included: Saturday 9:15am class at Camperdown. Wednesday 4pm slot to follow as
demand warrants. Coached by qualified, WWCC-checked trainers.

What's NOT included: Adult classes, nutrition (kids nutrition is parent-led).

Strategic notes: Pricing is sharp. Don't apologise for it — at $25/wk it's accessible and at par with
kids' soccer or swim school. The single biggest YTP marketing channel is parents who already
train at TOS. The single biggest YTP risk is mixed age range disappointing both ends of the
cohort — watch this in the first 6-8 weeks and split into 10-13 / 14-17 brackets if numbers
warrant.

YTP also unlocks a family revenue dynamic. A family with two kids in YTP and a parent in Squad
Ascent or SPT is paying $100-225/wk to The Outdoor Squad. That's the kind of LTV that builds a
business. Worth designing family bundle bonuses (not discounts) into the YTP page once it's
stable — e.g. parent of a YTP child gets free movement screen, free nutrition consult, etc., as add-
ons to their existing membership.

Squad Ascent — $51/wk

Role in the ladder: The core product. The membership that defines The Outdoor Squad as a
business.


--- PAGE 5 ---
Who it's for: Avatars 1 and 3 primarily — Desk-Bound Professionals and Holistic Movers. Avatar
2 (Strength Seekers) may start here before upgrading to SPT.
What's included: Unlimited group classes across all class types (Strength'N'Tone, HiiT'N'Run,
Buff'N'Puff, Flow'N'Flex, Outdoor Hyrox, Core'N'Sore) at both locations. Flow'N'Flex is the umbrella class for yoga, Pilates and mobility-style sessions.
What's NOT included: Personalised programming, nutrition coaching, formal movement screen,
regular 1:1 assessment, SPT slots, 1:1 PT.
Strategic notes: This is the product that the brand's reputation is built on. It carries the largest
volume of members and provides the community foundation that makes the rest of the offer
ladder make sense. Don't mess with it. Don't discount it. Don't add complexity that dilutes
"$51/wk for unlimited group." The simplicity is part of the value.
Squad Ascent is also where the long-term member relationship is forged. SPT members come
and go through life seasons; Squad Ascent members stay for years. The retention of this product
is the retention of the business.
SPT 2x + Group — $125/wk
Role in the ladder: The serious-trainee upgrade. Where members go when they want
personalisation, accountability, and real progression alongside the community they're already
part of.
Whoit's for: Avatar 4 (SPT Prospect) primarily — and the most common entry path is Sub-type
A (existing group member upgrading). Also Avatar 2 (Strength Seekers wanting structured
programming) and a slice of Avatar 3 (Holistic Movers wanting longevity-focused work).
What's included:

¢ 2SPT sessions per week (4-person max)

¢ Unlimited group classes (every Squad Ascent class included)

¢ Personalised programming on a 6-week cycle

¢ Movement screen and personalised warm-up (free, on starting)

e 12-week assessment cadence (regardless of join date)

¢ Nutrition plan and tracking

¢ Regular reviews and program adjustments
What's NOT included: 1:1 PT (different product), unlimited SPT slots (capped at 2/wk on this
tier).
Pricing logic: $125/wk = $62.50 per SPT session, with unlimited group bundled in at zero
additional cost. Compared to 1:1 PT at $150/session, SPT works out at less than half the per-

session rate while adding community, more sessions per week, and full programming
infrastructure. The pricing makes the upgrade decision easy for serious trainees.
Capacity note: 4-person max. This is the sweet spot — small enough to feel personal and deliver
real coaching attention, large enough to be commercially viable at this price point. Don't expand
to 5 or 6 — at that point the product becomes a small group class, not SPT, and the differentiation
collapses.
SPT 3x + Group — $175/wk
Role in the ladder: The committed-athlete tier. For members training seriously who want to
make SPT the foundation of their week.
Whoit's for: Avatar 2 (Strength Seekers), highly engaged Avatar 4 prospects, and members
specifically training for events (Hyrox, hikes, sport prep). Also a small subset of Avatar 3 who are
full-on about longevity and willing to invest in it.
What's included: Same as SPT 2x, but with 3 SPT sessions per week instead of 2.
Pricing logic: $175/wk = $58.30 per SPT session (with group bundled). The per-session rate goes
down as commitment goes up — the standard volume-discount logic of paid memberships.
Simple to explain: "more sessions, slightly less per session." Don't lose this — it's the rational
reason to step up.
Capacity note: As above. Same room, same trainer attention, just one more session per week for
the member.
28-Day Kickstarter — $397 total
Role in the ladder: SPT trial product. The most important new launch product because it's how
cold prospects experience SPT without committing to ongoing fees.
Whoit's for: Avatar 4 (SPT Prospect) primarily, especially Sub-types B (PT-curious newcomer)
and C (buddy/pair). Sub-type A (upgrading existing member) often skips this and goes straight
to ongoing SPT.
What's included:

¢ 4weeks of SPT (8 sessions if 2x/wk)

« Movement screen and personalised warm-up

¢ 28-day nutrition plan

¢ Initial assessment (baseline measurements, movement, key lifts)

« Finalassessment (progress data, program for next cycle)

¢ Unlimited group classes for the 28 days
What's NOT included: Ongoing membership (this is a trial / bridge product).
Pricing logic: $397 total for 28 days (do not describe this as $125/wk; $125/wk is SPT 2x + Group). Roughly 20% premium over SPT 2x at $125/wk standard

brainer "try before you commit" play that's profitable on its own.
Conversion target: Kickstarter members converting to ongoing SPT 2x or 3x at the end of the 28
days. Goal conversion rate: 70%+. Track this — it's the single most diagnostic metric of SPT
product-market fit.
Strategic notes: The Kickstarter is also the natural product to lead the launch campaign with
("Mates + Weights = Meights — try our 28-day SPT Kickstarter together"). It's a complete
product the prospect can evaluate, not a vague "join us" pitch.
For existing group members upgrading during the launch window: include launch bonuses
(longer pause window, free guest pass, complimentary follow-up nutrition consult, etc.) — value-
stack, not discount. Same $397 price; more in the box.
1:1 Personal Training — $150/session
Role in the ladder: Niche premium product. Available, not actively promoted.
Who it's for: Cases where SPT genuinely won't work — rehab clients, severe social anxiety
preventing group settings, very advanced or specialised athletic prep, members in crisis or
transition who need maximum personalisation. Also kept available for existing 1:1 PT clients who
don't want to switch.
What's included: One coaching session, fully personalised, any standard time/location.
Pricing logic: Lifted from $125 to $150 for two reasons. First, it makes the SPT comparison even
more obvious ($150 for one PT session vs $125/wk for two SPT sessions plus group). Second, $125
was under-market for an experienced trainer in the Inner West; $150 brings it in line.
Strategic positioning: When a prospect enquires about 1:1 PT, the conversation goes: "We do
offer 1:1 PT at $150 a session, and that's there for cases that genuinely need it. Most people who
come to us thinking they need 1:1 actually get better results from SPT — same level of
programming and attention, plus community, at half the per-session price. Worth me explaining
how SPT works first?"
That's the script. The bot should know it.
Strategic notes: 1:1 PT is being deliberately de-emphasised, not killed. Don't market it on the
website beyond a single line on the services page. Don't promote it in social. When prospects ask,

Operational policies that affect the offer ladder
Membership terms

e Squad Ascent, Squad Student, YTP: Weekly rolling. Cancel any time with 1 week's notice.

¢ SPT (any tier): 12-week minimum term, then fortnight-to-fortnight rolling.

¢ Drop-in: Per session, no commitment.

¢ 1:1 PT: Per session or in packs (define pack pricing if you offer them).

¢ 28-Day Kickstarter: Fixed 28-day term; converts to ongoing SPT or ends.
Pause policy (applies to all weekly memberships)

¢ Upto8 weeks per calendar year on hold

¢ Minimum 1-week blocks

e Requested in advance via email or in-app

¢ Nomid-block partial pauses
Cancellation and makeup (SPT-specific)

e 24hours' notice required for cancellation of an SPT session

« 1makeup session per calendar month allowed (use it or lose it; doesn't roll over)

¢ No-shows forfeit the session

¢ Rescheduling beyond 24 hours is at trainer's discretion
Trial-to-paid conversion windows

¢ 1-Day Free Trial Pass valid for one session within 14 days of issue

¢ Strong follow-up sequence (email + SMS) over the 14 days

¢ Hard nudge at day 7 and day 13 ("we'd love to see you back; here's how to make it happen")
Annual prepayment option (SPT only)
For SPT members specifically, an annual upfront payment option is available with a 5% discount.
The logic: this is a cash-flow-driven discount, not a price-competition discount. The 5% comes
from the cash flow benefit of upfront payment, not from caving to bargain-hunters. Annual
prepay also signals and reinforces member commitment, which improves retention.

The customer journey through the ladder
Most prospects don't enter and stay at one tier — they move through the ladder over time.
Designing the journey deliberately matters more than designing any single product well.
The standard adult journey (Avatar 1 / Avatar 3)
1. Awareness. Sees content (GBP, Pinterest, organic, word of mouth). Visits website.
2. Interest. Downloads meal plan OR books trial.
3. Trial. Comes to one session. Experiences the brand, the community, the format.
4. First commitment. Joins Squad Ascent at $51/wk.
5. Habituation. First 12 weeks. Most churn risk lives here. Trainer relationships, community
ties, visible progress.
6. Long-term member. Months 3-24. Squad Ascent regular. Possibly attends social events,
brings friends.
7. Optional upgrade. Specific trigger (goal, plateau, event, life change) causes interest in SPT.
Conversion to SPT 2x.
8. Long-term high-value member. SPT regular for 1-3 years. Possibly upgrades to SPT 3x.
Possibly downgrades back to Squad Ascent during life stages.
9. Lifetime advocate. Refers friends and family. Possibly enrolls a kid in YTP. May upgrade
partner via Meights to SPT.
The Strength Seeker journey (Avatar 2)
1. Awareness. Often arrives via Instagram, Reddit, word-of-mouth from another lifter.
2. Interest. Reads about programming, looks for credibility signals (kettlebell, strongman,
Hyrox content).

3. Trial OR direct entry. Often skips the trial — books the Kickstarter or signs up direct to
SPT.

4. Conversion. Almost immediately a high-value member. SPT 2x or 3x within first month.

5. Long-term high-value member. Stays for years if programming keeps them progressing.
The Parent-Buyer journey (Avatar 5)

1. Awareness. Often via existing parent network, school, or being a TOS member already.

2. Trial. Brings kid to a session (or watches a session) before committing.

3. Commitment. Enrols kid in YTP at $25/wk.

4. Family expansion. Possibly enrols second child. Possibly upgrades own membership.

5. Long-term family relationship. Multi-product household, often for years.
The Re-engagement journey (lapsed members)

1. Awareness. Receives outreach (Robo-Nick automation), or initiates contact themselves.

2. Re-trial. Often via Drop-in (paid one-off) or special "welcome back" trial.

3. Re-commitment. Squad Ascent or SPT depending on prior tier.
Strategic notes on the ladder as a system
Pricing logic in plain English

* Casual: $37/session (Drop-in)

« Group membership: ~$10/session at 5 sessions/wk ($51/wk Squad Ascent)

« SPT membership: ~$60/session including group bundled in

¢ Premium 1:1: $150/session
Each step up the ladder roughly doubles the price and adds a meaningful tier of personalisation,
programming, and accountability. That's how a good ladder feels — each step is a real choice, and
the value differences are obvious.
Why the SPT-as-2x-Group pricing works
SPT at $125/wk is roughly 2.5x Squad Ascent at $51/wk. That's a meaningful step but not an
unreachable one. For a member earning a professional salary, the upgrade decision is "do I want
personalised programming and accountability for an extra $74/wk?" — that's the right question


--- PAGE 11 ---
to be asking. Most "yes" answers come from members 6-24 months into their Squad Ascent
membership.
Don't compress this gap by introducing a mid-tier product. The clarity of "group OR SPT" is part
of why the ladder works.
Why we don't offer SPT-only (without group)
You could theoretically sell SPT 2x at $115/wk without group included. We're deliberately not
doing that. Reasons:
e It would create a weird ladder rung between Squad Ascent and SPT 2x
¢ It would dilute SPT's positioning (SPT is the upgrade — group is a feature, not optional)
¢ It would reduce session frequency for SPT members (they'd train 2x instead of 5x), which
would hurt their results, which would hurt retention, which would hurt the product
The "SPT + group bundled" architecture is a feature, not a constraint. It drives results and
revenue simultaneously.
Why 1:1 PT being de-emphasised matters more than killing it
Keeping 1:1 PT at $150/session and not actively promoting it does three jobs at once:
1. Anchors the price ladder — having a $150/session product available makes SPT look
extraordinary value
2. Serves edge cases — people who genuinely need 1:1 can still access it
3. Doesn't force existing 1:1 clients to switch — preserves their relationship with their trainer
4. Removes operational complexity by not needing a "1:1 PT marketing strategy" — the
strategy is "redirect to SPT unless absolutely necessary"
If 1:1 PT bookings drop to negligible levels over 12 months, you can quietly retire the product. If
they don't, you've Kept revenue you'd otherwise have lost.
What this ladder is missing (deliberately)
These are products you could add but shouldn't:
* Mid-tier semi-private (5-8 person small group). Compresses the SPT positioning. Don't.
¢ Online programming product. Different business model, different operations, would
distract from Bets 1 and 2.
¢ Corporate / team training. Possibly worth doing in year 2 once the core is stable. Not now.
¢ Anutrition-only product. Standalone hasn't worked; rolling it into SPT and Kickstarter is
the right path.

¢ Discount packages of any kind. Off-brand and erodes price integrity. Value-stack instead.

The website
Each product needs its own page or section, with a clear "who this is for" and clear progression to
the next step in the ladder. The website is currently being rebuilt as part of Bet 3 — this offer
architecture is the canonical product list for that rebuild.
The team
Trainers and the VA need to be able to talk about every product in the ladder confidently. A 30-
minute team session walking through this document is worthwhile. The most common confused
conversation will be "which product should I be on?" — every team member should be able to
handle that.
